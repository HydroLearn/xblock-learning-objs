Examples

Given a topographic dataset, the student will be able to implement the key steps in watershed delineation, including pitfilling, flow direction, flow accumulation, threshold assignment, and stream network extraction. [Applying]

Given a water supply and demand dataset, the student will be able to assess potential value of using non-traditional water resources to reduce water stresses, considering cost factors associated with the non-traditional resources [Analyzing]

Condition

The condition under which the student will perform the described behavior. Examples include:

  • Given a topographic dataset
  • Given a water supply and demand dataset,
  • Given a diagram of the water cycle,
  • Using historical rain gauge data from NOAA’s Precipitation Frequency Data Server (PFDS),
  • Using the alternating block method and Intensity-Duration-Frequency (IDF) curves,
  • Given instructions on how to use a script language,

Action verb + Object

A learning objective contains a verb that refers to actions associated with the intended learning level from Bloom’s Taxonomy, and an object that describes the knowledge students are expected to acquire or construct. Examples include:

  • Implement the key steps in watershed delineation
  • Assess potential value of using non-traditional water resources to reduce water stresses
  • Interpret at least three sources of ground water recharge
  • Develop Depth-Duration-Frequency (DDF) curves
  • Develop a design-storm hyetograph
  • Construct a simple NWM-based flood warning tool

Bloom's Taxonomy Learning Levels (with examples of action verbs)

Level Description Example Action Verbs
Level (1): Remembering Retrieve relevant knowledge from long-term memory in the approximate form in which they were learned. define, describe, identify, label, list, match, name, order, recall, select
Level (2): Understanding Construct meaning from instructional messages. Translate, comprehend, or interpret information based on prior learning illustrate, compare, distinguish, estimate, explain, classify, interpret, predict, summarize
Level (3): Applying Carry out or use a procedure in a given situation. Select and use data and principles to complete a problem or task with a minimum of direction organize, solve, demonstrate, discover, modify, operate, predict, prepare, produce, relate, solve, implement
Level (4): Analyzing Break material into constituent parts and determine how parts relate to one another and to an overall structure or purpose. Distinguish, classify, and relate assumptions, hypotheses, evidence, or structure of a statement or question analyze, break down, compare, select, contrast, deconstruct, discriminate, distinguishes, identify, outline
Level (5): Evaluating Make judgments based on criteria or standards. Appraise, assess, or critique on a basis of specific standards and criteria rank, assess, monitor, check, test, judge, appraise, criticize, defend, conclude, critique, justify, support
Level (6): Creating Put elements together to form a coherent or functional whole; reorganize elements into a new pattern or structure. Originate, integrate, and combines ideas into a product, plan, or proposal that is new to learner generate, plan, compose, develop, create, invent, organize, construct, produce, compile, design, devise

Degree

Describe the degree or level of performance. Examples include:

  • including pitfilling, flow direction, flow accumulation, threshold assignment, and stream network extraction
  • considering cost factors associated with the non-traditional resources
  • with a definition of each source and description of how it functions in the larger water cycle
  • for at least 5 return periods between 2-year to 100-year
  • for a prescribed temporal interval
  • with the ability to notify users when the NWM predicts above average streamflows in a particular region

ABET Outcomes

Optionally, you can also provide associated ABET. The following listing defines the expected items.

  • An ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems by applying principles of engineering, science, and mathematics
  • An ability to apply engineering design to produce solutions that meet specified needs with consideration of public health, safety, and welfare, as well as global, cultural, social, environmental, and economic factors
  • An ability to communicate effectively with a range of audiences
  • An ability to recognize ethical and professional responsibilities in engineering situations and make informed judgments, which must consider the impact of engineering solutions in global, economic, environmetal, and societal contexts
  • An ability to function effectively on a team whose members together provide leadership, create a collaborative and inclusive environmetna, establish goals, plan tasks, and meet objectives
  • An ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation, analyze and interpret data, and use engineering judgement to draw conclusions
  • An ability to acquire and apply new knowledge as needed, using appropriate learning strategies
